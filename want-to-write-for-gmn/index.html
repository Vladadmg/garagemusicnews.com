Want to Write for GMN?

What We Want:

By 3/15 for an issue themed “Progress”:

-A band that’d like to be interviewed before April about their recent progress.

-Short reviews of albums that show progress for a band (local or national), between 100-200 words.

-Short write-ups about news in the music world 200-300 words. Brief structure for news articles can be found here.

-A playlist (8-12 songs), featuring music to help us all get over cold weather, with a short intro about the theme and how the playlists fits.

To send us something listed above:

-Put the draft in Google Docs or Microsoft Word (so we can give you feedback or any edits to make).

-Email us the draft or link to the Google Doc draft at garagemusicnews@gmail.com, Subject: Submission: [the thing you’re responding to]

-In the email include a very short bio: <50 words. Find examples on our Contributors page.

 

How to Pitch a Story:

If there’s a type of article you’d like to do that’s not in our “What We Want” then:

–Here are some examples of what we publish.

-Send us an email at garagemusicnews@gmail.com

-In the email include:

1) A possible title.

2) The idea. It won’t be possible to explain everything, or mention every detail, so try to focus on what’s most important. Think about what question your article would answer.

3) Why you are a good person to write the piece. You might have a personal connection to the subject, or your experience might give you something unique to say.

4) What you would do – where would you get your info? Other news reports, interviews?

5) Finally, explain what kind of article this should be. Would it be short or long? A feature article, a news item, an interview, a review, or something else?

-Make sure it’s 75-150 words

-Or send us a draft of a piece.

-Or come to our meetings and pitch in person and work with other writers to make it happen!

 

Spring Meetings:

Our meetings are two hours and have a few segments. Sometimes there’s a brief writing lesson about something that’s tripping up a lot of our writers. We have our editorial meeting where we pitch ideas and plan upcoming issues. Then there’s work time so you can brainstorm with other writers or get feedback from our editor. If you have a computer you can bring it, otherwise we have a few available. Some of our writers prefer pen and paper which is great, too!

While The Garage is being renovated meetings will be held in St. Paul at the Mid-Continent Oceanographic Institute, formerly known as Rock Star Supply Co. They’re an organization that tutors and teaches writing to young people.
